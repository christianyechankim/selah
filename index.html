Founding vision presentation

Church Musician
Network Blueprint

A private membership platform and Slack-based ministry community designed to strengthen worship teams through formation, training, prayer, and partnership.

Core idea

Build a ministry home, not a resource dump

The website should anchor identity, doctrine, archives, and onboarding. Slack should handle the daily life of the network: prayer, questions, needs, encouragement, and peer support.

Website

Formation

Vision, history, teaching library, seminar archive, onboarding, profiles.

Slack

Community

Instrument rooms, care, church needs, leader collaboration, quick support.

This two-part model keeps the mission intentional while still creating a living, relational network.

Leadership model

Each channel needs a prayerful point person

Role

Channel lead

A one-year servant leader responsible for warmth, order, and follow-through.

Calling

Ministry posture

Prayerful selection, doctrinal trust, humility, and relational maturity matter as much as skill.

Support

Core team backing

Leads should never moderate alone; escalation and care must flow to a central leadership team.

Lead responsibilities

Keep expectations clear and lightweight

  • Welcome new members into the channel.
  • Answer questions or route them to the right person.
  • Post one prompt, tip, or resource each week.
  • Model prayer, humility, and encouragement.
  • Surface church needs, care issues, or conflict to core leadership.
  • Join monthly leader check-ins and annual review.

Onboarding journey

The first week should feel guided and personal

Approve application

Send welcome email with login and Start Here instructions.

Complete profile

Church, city, instrument, experience, availability, and prayer needs.

Join Slack and introduce yourself

Post in #start-here and select the right instrument room.

Receive a welcome from the channel lead

Immediate personal connection moves members from browsing to belonging.

Launch sequence

Run the first 90 days in three phases

Days 1–30

Foundation

Finalize mission, covenant, pages, channel setup, welcome video, and first-month content.

Days 31–60

Pilot

Invite a trusted founding cohort, test onboarding, host orientation, and refine weak spots.

Days 61–90

Public launch

Open applications, begin rhythms, activate church-needs support, and review engagement data.

Operating rhythm

Consistency will matter more than volume

Weekly

Prayer thread

Shared care and follow-up.

Weekly

Instrument prompt

One useful discussion in each room.

Monthly

Workshop or seminar

Live training or replay upload.

Quarterly

Review and adjust

Survey, channel health, lead support.

Build the website, recruit channel leads, launch a guided pilot, and let culture form before scale. The strength of the network will come from trust, prayer, and steady rhythms.
